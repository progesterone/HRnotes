# Biography
Albert Hofmann, born January 11th, 1906 in Baden, Aargau, Switzerland is a known Swiss chemist, best know for first synthesizing and consuming [[LSD|LSD-25]]. Him and his team also synthesized and produced [[Psilocybin]] and it's cousin [[Psilocin]] . 

## Early Life and Education
Coming from a low income household, he was forced to work as his father fell ill. With help from a godfather, who paid for his education, he aimed to study chemistry within plants and animals along side with Paul Karrer at the university of Zurich. In just 4 years he graduated with a doctorate in 1929, studying the chemical structure of Chitin within animals.

# Career
Originally with the intention of pursuing a artistic career, he was enthralled by theoretical knowledge problems within chemistry. Immediately after graduation, he was hired at Sandoz Laboratories to study for a respiratory and a circulatory stimulant. On the 16th of November, 1938, he first synthesized LSD. However, not proving to be what he was looking for, he set it aside. 5 years later, he accidently consumed LSD, on the 16th of April 1943. 

Three days later, 19th of April, 1943, he intentionally ingested 250 micrograms of LSD, with the original intent of a small dose. However 250 micrograms proved to be a strong experience as he rode his bicycle around during the experiment. This day will become known as `Bicycle Day`. 
![[Albert Hofmann Notes#House of Switzerland.org]]

The discovery of LSD infleunced several psychiatrists, notable Ronald, A. Sandison. Who developed and showed successful progress in psychotherapy which gained international acclaim.

Later within his career, he discovered [[4-acetoxy-DET]], a hallucinogenic tryptamine in 1958. Following his discovery, he studied mushrooms found in Mexico with psychedelic properties, later identifying [[Psilocybin]]. During his trip, he unsuccessfully identified the mechanisms of Salvia Divinorum.

Also credited to discovering Methergine, a drug used to treat postpartum hemorrhaging, and investigating and researching peyote, a [[Mescaline]] derivative.

# Later In Life
On his 100th birthday, he gave a speech where he described LSD and it's use as a "medicine for the soul." With other key notes of frustration with worldwide prohibition, blaming the misuse in the countercultures of the 1960s, and criticized unfair political establishments of the day.
